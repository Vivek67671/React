Budget Management: A UX Case Study

Project Overview

Designing a seamless budget management experience for organizations using multiple cloud providers. The case study focuses on solving the challenges of tracking, allocating, and controlling cloud spend across platforms.

[Cloud Budget Management Illustration]

Introduction

With the growing adoption of cloud services, organizations increasingly use multiple cloud providers for redundancy, cost optimization, and leveraging unique features. This brings challenges in tracking and managing budgets across providers.

[Cloud Budget Management Illustration]

Problem Statement

DevOps Managers need to accurately forecast and allocate cloud budgets in multi-cloud environments, but projections often miss critical factors, making budget management difficult.

Goal

Design user experience for technical personas (FinOps/DevOps Managers/CTO) to create, track, and understand budgets for multiple cloud platforms.

Understanding and Research

Interviews with clients revealed the need for clarity on budget health, customizable alerts, and access to trends for informed decisions.

Initial Learning

  • No clarity on budget health status
  • Need for customizable thresholds for alerts
  • Trends help stakeholders make decisions

Ideation Process

Mapped user flows, sketched layouts, and identified core features. Split features into two main sections to address user goals and pain points.

[Sketches and Wireframes]

Design Iteration 1

Delivered a dashboard to display current spending, create budgets, and configure alerts. Easy-to-read graphs and pre-set alerts help users assess budget health.

[Dashboard and Graphs]

Design Iteration 2

Updated design for multi-cloud support, improved budget period display, dynamic preview section, and refined alert configuration.
